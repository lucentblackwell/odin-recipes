Kimchi (Korean Fermented Cabbage)

Korea.net / Korean Culture and Information Service (Photographer name), CC BY-SA 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Description

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish made from salted and fermented vegetables, most commonly napa cabbage, seasoned with a vibrant mixture of chili powder, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce.

Ingredients

  • Napa Cabbage: 1 large head (cut into quarters)
  • Coarse Salt: 1 cup (for brining)
  • Water: Enough to cover the cabbage
  • Kimchi Paste:
    • Korean Chili Flakes (Gochugaru): 1/2 cup
    • Fish Sauce: 1/4 cup
    • Chopped garlic: 1 tablespoon

Steps

  1. Rub the coarse salt liberally onto every leaf of the cabbage quarters.

  2. Place the salted cabbage in a large bowl, cover with water, and let it sit for 2-3 hours. The cabbage should wilt and become pliable.

  3. Thoroughly rinse the cabbage under cold water 2-3 times to remove excess salt. Squeeze out the water and let it drain for about 30 minutes

  4. In a separate bowl, mix the Korean Chili Flakes (Gochugaru), Fish Sauce, and chopped Garlic to form the kimchi paste.

  5. Wear gloves! Rub the kimchi paste onto every leaf of the prepared cabbage quarters. Ensure the paste is evenly distributed.

  6. Tightly pack the kimchi into an airtight glass jar. Leave a little space at the top.

  7. Leave the jar at room temperature for 1-2 days to kickstart fermentation (the kimchi will begin to smell sour and produce bubbles).

  8. Transfer the jar to the refrigerator. Kimchi is ready to eat immediately but will continue to ferment and develop deeper, sour flavors over weeks.
