Paneer Tikka Masala

Srivalli Vijayakumar, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Description

Paneer Tikka Masala is a popular North Indian dish featuring marinated and grilled cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) simmered in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-based gravy.

Ingredients

  • Paneer: 200g, cubed
  • Yogurt: 1/2 cup (for marination)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Tomato Puree: 1 cup
  • Cream: 1/4 cup
  • Spices: 1 teaspoon of
    • Garam Masala
    • Turmeric Powder
    • Red Chili Powder

Steps

  1. Mix the paneer cubes with yogurt, half of the ginger-garlic paste, and a pinch of all three spices (Garam Masala, Turmeric, Chili Powder). Marinate for 30 minutes.

  2. Grill, pan-fry, or bake the marinated paneer until the cubes have light brown/charred edges. Set aside.

  3. In a pan, heat a little oil. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Add the remaining spices and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

  5. Pour in the tomato puree (or crushed tomatoes) and cook until the oil starts to separate from the sides (about 10-15 minutes).

  6. Add about 1/2 cup of water (or broth) to adjust the gravy consistency. Bring to a simmer.

  7. Stir in the cream and add the cooked paneer tikka pieces. Gently simmer for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.
